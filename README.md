# Projects
**- Stochastic Tuning and Chemoresistance Simulation**

Computational modeling of RNA and protein patterns in response to various drug concentrations to analyze the stochastic tuning of transcriptional noise and subsequent resistance protein production in a tumor population of cells. Using a Monte Carlo continuous time simulation from the Gillespie algorithm, the RNA and protein production behavior of a single cell and cell survival in a population of 50-100 cells were simultaneously tracked to understand how cell health is randomly optimized as a stress response against conditions that are not accounted for in the genetic programming.

# Research
**Genome Evolution of Eukaryotic Transposases**

Using local and global alignment tools like Diamond, BLAST, and MAFFT with tree-making software like IQ-Tree, relationships between species with specific transposases responsible for inherent gene-editing systems like the PiggyBac transposase in ciliates are analyzed. This project involves identifying putative mobile genetic elements (MGEs) and mapping novel relationships using phylogenomics, bioinformatics, and high-performance computing. Ciliate genome biology acts as a blueprint to explore the evolution of domesticated MGEs in diverse eukaryotes that are harnessed as biotechnological tools that contribute to immunity, survival, and reproduction.

**Identifying DksA-Dependent Candidate Genes as Regulators of _E. coli_ cell division**

Identified genes that regulate cell division in bacteria with the stress signaling molecule ppGpp and its complementary transcription factor DksA. These two molecules indirectly activate an important component of the divisome responsible for cell division: FtsZ. Promising genes were deleted to create mutant strains with the heat-sensitive allele _ftsZ84_, and heat sensitivity assays were performed to analyze the effect of the absence of the potential genes on cell division. Gene deletions were confirmed using Polymerase Chain Reaction (PCR). Results contribute to understanding how bacteria regulate cell division in unfavorable conditions, which extend to a broader group of pathogenic bacteria in the presence of antibiotic efforts.
